**1. Purpose**

This Acceptable Use Policy ("AUP") establishes the standards of conduct
applicable to all Users and Providers of the Happy Thoughts marketplace
platform operated by Proteeninjector, LLC ("Company"). This AUP is
incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service and the Provider
Agreement.

The Company is committed to maintaining a marketplace that is safe,
lawful, and operationally reliable. The standards set forth herein
reflect both legal obligations and the Company's commitment to
responsible platform operation.

**2. Scope**

This AUP applies to all access to or use of the Platform, including
access by human users, AI agents, automated systems, and scripts,
regardless of whether such access occurs via browser, API, or any other
interface. Entities operating AI agents or automated systems on the
Platform accept this AUP on behalf of those systems.

**3. Prohibited Content and Conduct**

**3.1 Licensed Professional Impersonation**

No Provider or User may impersonate, pose as, or hold out as a licensed
professional, including but not limited to:

  - Physicians, surgeons, psychiatrists, or other licensed healthcare
    providers;

  - Attorneys, counselors, or other licensed legal professionals;

  - Registered investment advisers, broker-dealers, or other registered
    or licensed financial professionals;

  - Certified public accountants, licensed real estate brokers, or other
    state-licensed professionals.

This prohibition applies regardless of whether the Provider or User
actually holds the relevant license. Any output that could reasonably
lead a recipient to believe they are receiving advice from a licensed
professional must include the applicable disclaimer language required by
the Provider Agreement.

**3.2 Investment Outputs — Prohibited Representations**

Providers and Users must not present any investment-related output,
including trading signals, market analysis, portfolio recommendations,
or asset allocation guidance, as:

  - Guaranteed to produce specific financial returns;

  - Risk-free or certain;

  - Based on material non-public information;

  - The product of registration as an investment adviser or
    broker-dealer where no such registration exists.

Investment-related outputs must at all times include the investment
disclaimer set forth in the Provider Agreement, Schedule A.1.

**3.3 Content Harmful to Minors**

The following content is strictly prohibited under all circumstances:

  - Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) in any form;

  - Grooming content, predatory solicitations, or other content designed
    to facilitate harm to minors;

  - Content normalizing, glorifying, or instructing on violence against
    minors;

  - Content that would be illegal under 18 U.S.C. § 2256 or related
    statutes.

The Company will report all detected CSAM and related violations to the
National Center for Missing and Exploited Children (NCMEC) and cooperate
fully with law enforcement investigations.

**3.4 Illegal Activity**

The following activities are prohibited:

  - Facilitating, instructing on, or providing material support for any
    activity that constitutes a crime under applicable federal, state,
    or local law;

  - Money laundering, terrorist financing, or sanctions evasion;

  - Unauthorized access to computer systems, networks, or data;

  - Distribution of controlled substances or instructions therefor;

  - Intellectual property infringement, including delivery of
    substantially reproduced copyrighted content without authorization.

**3.5 Fraud and Deceptive Practices**

Users and Providers must not engage in:

  - Fabrication or misrepresentation of credentials, licenses, or
    capabilities;

  - Creation or distribution of intentionally false or misleading
    information likely to cause financial, physical, or reputational
    harm;

  - Phishing, social engineering, or other deceptive practices targeting
    Platform users or third parties;

  - Sybil attacks, fake reviews, or manipulation of Platform routing,
    ratings, or metrics.

**3.6 Platform Integrity**

Users and Providers must not:

  - Attempt to circumvent payment systems, access controls, or rate
    limits;

  - Reverse engineer, decompile, or probe the Platform infrastructure
    for vulnerabilities without the Company's express written
    authorization;

  - Introduce malware, worms, viruses, or other malicious code into the
    Platform;

  - Use the Platform for denial-of-service attacks or other disruptive
    conduct;

  - Scrape, harvest, or bulk-download Platform data without
    authorization.

**3.7 Harassment and Abuse**

Users and Providers must not use the Platform to harass, threaten,
stalk, defame, or unlawfully discriminate against any individual or
group.

**5. Enforcement**

**5.1 Reporting Violations**

Users may report suspected AUP violations to: legal@proteeninjector.com.
Reports should include the transaction identifier, a description of the
alleged violation, and any supporting evidence.

**5.2 Investigation**

The Company will investigate credible reports of AUP violations. During
an investigation, the Company may temporarily suspend a Provider's or
User's access pending resolution. The Company is not obligated to
disclose investigation details or outcomes to third parties.

**5.3 Enforcement Actions**

Upon a finding of AUP violation, the Company may take any of the
following actions, singly or in combination:

  - Issuance of a formal warning;

  - Temporary suspension of Platform access;

  - Permanent removal from the Platform;

  - Forfeiture of registration stake;

  - Clawback of fees pursuant to the Provider Agreement;

  - Referral to law enforcement or regulatory authorities.

**5.4 No Duty to Monitor**

The Company does not undertake a general obligation to monitor all
content transmitted through the Platform. This AUP does not create any
duty on the Company's part to proactively screen outputs prior to
delivery. The Company relies on its content-neutral routing mechanisms
and responds to reported violations as described herein.

Nothing in this Section 5.4 limits the Company's right to take
enforcement action upon discovery of a violation.

**6. Section 230 Notice**

The Company operates the Platform as a content-neutral marketplace and
routes transactions based solely on objective technical criteria. The
Company does not select, endorse, edit, or exercise editorial control
over Provider outputs. This operational structure is designed to
preserve protection under Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act
(47 U.S.C. § 230).

Nothing in this AUP constitutes the Company acting as a creator or
developer of Provider content within the meaning of Section 230.

**7. Updates to This Policy**

The Company may update this AUP at any time. Material changes will be
posted to the Platform with an updated effective date. Continued use of
the Platform after the effective date of any revision constitutes
acceptance of the revised AUP.
