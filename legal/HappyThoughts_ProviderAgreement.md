**5. Fee Structure and Payment**

Provider will receive seventy percent (70%) of the transaction amount
for each completed Thought delivery, transmitted directly to Provider's
registered wallet address via the x402 protocol. The Company retains

Payments are transmitted automatically upon successful completion of a
transaction as recorded by the Platform. The Company does not hold or
custody Provider funds at any point during the transaction lifecycle.

In the event of a transaction that is subsequently reversed, disputed,
or found to involve fraudulent activity, the Company reserves the right
to withhold or claw back amounts from Provider's future earnings as
described in Section 6.

**6. Fee Clawback Rights**

The Company reserves the right to claw back or offset previously
transmitted fees or future earnings in the following circumstances:

**6.1 Misrepresentation Clawback**

If Provider materially misrepresented its credentials, specialties, or
capabilities at registration or in any re-attestation, the Company may
recover all platform fees paid to Provider from the date of
misrepresentation through the date of discovery, up to a maximum of
twelve (12) months of earnings.

**6.2 Policy Violation Clawback**

If Provider delivered outputs that violated the Acceptable Use Policy or
applicable law, the Company may recover fees associated with those
specific transactions, plus an administrative penalty not to exceed the
lesser of: (a) one hundred (100) USDC; or (b) the total fees earned by
Provider in the thirty (30) days preceding the violation.

**6.3 Dispute-Related Clawback**

If a transaction is successfully disputed by a Buyer due to Provider
failure to deliver promised output, the Company may claw back the
Provider's share of that transaction and refund the Buyer.

**6.4 Clawback Process**

The Company will provide written notice of any intended clawback action
with a fifteen (15) business day cure or response period before
execution, except where immediate action is required to prevent ongoing
fraud or harm.
