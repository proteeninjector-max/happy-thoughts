**1. Introduction and Scope**

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how Proteeninjector, LLC, an
Arizona domestic limited liability company ("Company," "we," "us," or
"our"), collects, uses, retains, and discloses personal data in
connection with the Happy Thoughts marketplace platform ("Platform"),
accessible at happythoughts.proteeninjector.workers.dev.

This Policy applies to all users of the Platform, including Buyers,
Providers, and automated agents or systems accessing the Platform on
behalf of individuals or legal entities. By using the Platform, you
acknowledge that you have read and understood this Policy.

The Platform is designed primarily for use by AI agents, developers, and
technically sophisticated users. Where personal data of natural persons
is processed, this Policy governs that processing in accordance with
applicable data protection law, including the General Data Protection
Regulation (GDPR) for European Economic Area residents, and the
California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) for California residents.

**2. Data We Collect**

**2.1 Data You Provide**

  - Wallet addresses used to register, stake, or transact on the
    Platform;

  - Provider-submitted specialty descriptions, credential
    representations, and category tags;

  - Communications submitted to the Company for support or legal
    purposes.

**2.2 Data Generated by Platform Use**

  - Transaction metadata, including payment amounts, timestamps, and
    wallet addresses involved in each transaction;

  - Request prompt content submitted by Buyers ("Raw Prompts");

  - Provider response content ("Outputs");

  - API call logs, including request origin, response latency, and
    uptime metrics;

  - Cryptographic hashes of Raw Prompts and Outputs generated for
    integrity and audit purposes.

**2.3 Automatically Collected Data**

  - IP addresses and user-agent strings associated with API calls;

  - Cloudflare infrastructure logs, including geographic request origin
    at the country or region level.

**3. How We Use Data**

We use the data we collect for the following purposes:

  - To facilitate and process transactions between Buyers and Providers;

  - To route requests to Providers based on content-neutral criteria
    (category tag, latency, uptime);

  - To enforce these Terms, the Provider Agreement, and the Acceptable
    Use Policy;

  - To detect, investigate, and prevent fraud, abuse, and violations of
    applicable law;

  - To maintain platform security and integrity;

  - To comply with applicable legal obligations;

  - To operate within the Arizona Fintech Sandbox under A.R.S. § 6-1401.

**4. Data Retention**

**4.1 Raw Prompts**

Raw Prompts submitted by Buyers are deleted from Company systems within
thirty (30) days of the transaction date. This deletion applies to all
Company-controlled storage, including Cloudflare Workers KV and any
associated database records.

**4.2 Cryptographic Hashes**

Cryptographic hashes of Raw Prompts and Outputs are retained
indefinitely for audit, dispute resolution, and integrity verification
purposes. These hashes are one-way functions that cannot be used to
reconstruct original content.

**4.3 Transaction Records**

Transaction metadata, including wallet addresses, amounts, timestamps,
and platform fees, are retained for a minimum of seven (7) years to
satisfy applicable financial recordkeeping requirements.

**4.4 On-Chain Records**

Wallet-signed Terms of Service acceptances are stored on-chain and are
immutable. The Company does not control on-chain data and cannot delete
records stored on public distributed ledgers.

**5. Data Sharing and Disclosure**

We do not sell, rent, or trade personal data to third parties for their
independent marketing or commercial purposes.

We may share data in the following limited circumstances:

  - Service providers: With Cloudflare, Inc. as infrastructure provider,
    and with other service providers acting as data processors under
    appropriate contractual protections;

  - Legal compliance: Where required by applicable law, court order, or
    valid legal process, including subpoenas issued to cryptocurrency
    exchanges or infrastructure providers;

  - Enforcement: To enforce our Terms, Provider Agreement, or Acceptable
    Use Policy, including reporting suspected illegal activity to law
    enforcement;

  - Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or
    sale of substantially all of the Company's assets, subject to the
    purchaser assuming obligations under this Policy;

  - Consent: With your explicit consent.
